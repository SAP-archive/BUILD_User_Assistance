Feedback Studies

The SPLASH Feedback tool lets you create and share feedback studies to gather detailed feedback from people about images, project pages, or interactive HTML prototype applications.

To start, you create a new study, give it a name, and then add images or HTML prototypes to it. Then enter questions to be answered about each image, and create tasks for participants to complete on HTML prototypes. Then you publish the study to generate a study-link, which you send to people that you want to provide feedback.

When study participants click the study-link, the study opens in their browser. If they don't have a SPLASH account, the SPLASH landing page is displayed and you can sign up.When the study is displayed, participants have the option to keep their responses anonymous. As participants enter their responses, the project team members can view the feedback on the Feedback results page page almost immediately.

Note

User research studies are created within a project. Therefore you must create a project, or be a member of project , before you can create a user research study. For more information, see Create a New Project

  1. Sign in to SPLASH.
  2. Open a project.
  3. Create and publish a feedback study.
  4. Invite people to provide feedback.
  5. SPLASH sends a study-link to each person, and they open the link in their browsers and provide feedback.
  6. You and other project team members can view the participants' feedback on the Feedback results page of the project.
